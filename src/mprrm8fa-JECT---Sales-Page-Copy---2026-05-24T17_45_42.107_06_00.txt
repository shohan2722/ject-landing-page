SECTION 12: FAQ — OBJECTION HANDLING
(Address the top hesitations before they become reasons not to book)




Frequently Asked Questions


Q: I'm nervous about looking overdone or unnatural. How does JECT approach that?


This is the concern we hear most — and honestly, it's the concern that drove the development of our entire approach. Every JECT provider is trained in the Academy-Trained Micro-Dose Method: a personalized, anatomy-first technique that prioritizes subtle, natural-looking results over volume or dramatic change. Before any treatment, your provider assesses your facial anatomy, understands your goals, and creates a plan specific to you. If you don't want anyone to know, they won't.




Q: Are JECT providers licensed and medically trained?


Yes. Every JECT provider is a licensed medical professional who completes JECT Academy — a comprehensive training program developed with JECT's Medical Directors specifically for our standards and techniques. Medical Director oversight is part of our operational structure, not just a credential we list on a website.




Q: I don't know which treatment is right for me. Where do I start?


That's exactly what your consultation is for. You don't need to arrive with a treatment decision already made. Your JECT provider will ask about your goals, concerns, skin history, upcoming events, and lifestyle — and build a personalized recommendation from there. Some clients need one service; others benefit from a multi-step plan. We'll help you understand the options clearly before you commit to anything.




Q: How long do results last?


It varies by treatment. Wrinkle reducers typically last 3–4 months. Dermal fillers can last 6–18 months depending on the product and area. Laser treatments and microneedling produce collagen-stimulating effects that continue to develop and can last 12 months or longer. Your provider will give you realistic expectations for your specific treatment at consultation.




Q: What if I've had Botox or filler before and I'm unhappy with the results?


It happens — and it's often the result of an approach that wasn't calibrated to your anatomy or goals. At JECT, your provider will assess your current baseline before making any recommendations. We'll discuss what you liked, what you didn't, and build a plan that addresses both. You don't need to be perfect going in.




Q: Is there downtime after treatments?


It varies significantly by treatment. Wrinkle reducers and lighter injectables generally have minimal downtime — most clients return to work or social plans the same day. Laser treatments like Moxi or BBL may involve a few days of redness or skin shedding. Microneedling may produce 24–48 hours of pinkness. Your provider will walk you through the expected recovery timeline before you commit to any treatment — especially important if you're planning around an event.




Q: Can I bring a friend or partner to my consultation?


Absolutely. Many clients bring a trusted friend, partner, or family member — especially for their first visit. Your consultation space is comfortable and private, and your provider is happy to answer questions from anyone you bring.




Q: How does JECT's referral program work?


When you refer a friend to JECT, you receive a $50 credit toward your next treatment or product purchase once they complete their first appointment. There's no limit to the number of referrals you can make or the credits you can accumulate. You'll find referral links and details in your client account.




Q: What skincare products does JECT recommend?


JECT's medical-grade skincare shop carries a curated selection of clinically effective products across cleansers, retinols, vitamin C serums, moisturizers, sunscreens, and correctives. All recommendations come directly from your provider based on your treatment plan and skin goals. Many clients combine in-clinic treatments with home skincare protocols for accelerated results.




Q: How do I book?


Online, in under two minutes. Select your location, your treatment or consultation type, and choose an available time that works for you. No phone calls required. You'll receive confirmation and pre-appointment information via email and SMS.

Mobile vs. Desktop Optimization Strategies
Mobile-Specific Requirements:
* Headlines: Maximum 7 words on mobile viewport — use line breaks strategically
* Subheadlines: Single complete sentence, never orphaned single words on last line
* CTAs: Minimum 48px tap target height, full-width buttons, high-contrast color (JECT brand palette)
* Testimonials: Card-style with avatar, scrollable carousel format on mobile
* Service sections: Accordion-style expandable on mobile to reduce scroll length
* Images: Webp format, lazy loading, max 200kb per hero image
* Font size: Minimum 16px body text on mobile — never smaller
* Paragraph length: Maximum 3 lines before a visual break on mobile
* Trust bar: Icon-only format on smallest viewports, text visible on medium+


Desktop-Specific Optimization:
* Two-column layout for services and testimonials at 1024px+
* Sidebar sticky CTA visible on scroll for high-intent visitors
* Expanded FAQ with visible answers (no accordion required on desktop)
* Location map integration for multi-location selection
* Before/after image gallery with hover states and demographic filters




Visual Hierarchy Recommendations
* H1 Headline: Largest type on page, high-contrast, top of viewport
* Subheadline: Second hierarchy, lighter weight, clearly supporting headline
* CTA Buttons: JECT brand color (primary), high contrast against background, consistent sizing
* Section headlines: Clear visual separation with whitespace, not just font size
* Testimonials: Visually differentiated with quote marks, avatar, name, and location
* Trust signals: Consistent icon treatment throughout page, never overwhelming
* Offer callouts: Boxed, background color differentiated, visually separate from body copy

Results Presentation Guidelines
Always frame results as:
* Realistic and achievable ("most clients," "typically," "commonly")
* Personalized ("results vary based on individual anatomy and treatment plan")
* Process-oriented ("over the course of your treatment plan," "after your full series")
* Experiential as well as physical ("felt more confident," "noticed a difference in how I carry myself")


Never frame results as:
* Guaranteed outcomes
* Universal ("everyone who does X gets Y")
* Extreme transformations presented as typical
* Before/after comparisons without context (lighting, timing, editing disclosure)
